Why Walking Is the Best Exercise (Especially When You Hate Exercise)

By Mia Reynolds · December 31, 2024 · 7 min read

I have a gym membership I've used exactly twice in the past year. I own workout videos I'll "definitely do tomorrow." I bought a yoga mat that my cat sleeps on. Exercise and I have a complicated relationship.

But walking? Walking I can do. Walking doesn't require special skills or coordination (I have neither). It doesn't require childcare or a class schedule. I can do it pushing a stroller, with a kid on a bike, or alone with a podcast and my thoughts.

Here's what I've learned: the best exercise is the one you'll actually do. And for me - and maybe for you - that's walking.

Why Walking Works When Nothing Else Sticks

Tip #1: Start embarrassingly small
10 minutes around the block counts. You don't have to hit 10,000 steps on day one. Build the habit first, increase later.
Tip #2: Attach it to something enjoyable
I only let myself listen to my favorite true crime podcast while walking. Now I actually WANT to go. Find your "walking treat."
Tip #3: Make it convenient
Shoes by the door. Phone charged. Belt bag packed. The fewer decisions you have to make, the more likely you are to go.
Tip #4: Walk early if you can
Every hour that passes is another chance for something to "come up." Morning walks get done. Evening walks get cancelled.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many steps should I walk per day?

10,000 steps is the common goal, but research shows significant health benefits starting at just 4,000 steps per day. Start where you are and add 500-1,000 steps per week. Consistency matters more than hitting a magic number.

Is walking as good as running?

For cardiovascular health, brisk walking provides similar benefits to running with significantly less joint stress. Walking is more sustainable long-term for most people, which means you're more likely to stick with it.

Do compression socks help with walking?

Yes! Compression socks improve blood circulation and can reduce leg fatigue, especially on longer walks. Many people find they can walk farther, feel less tired, and recover faster when wearing compression.

What should I carry on a walk?

Essentials: phone, keys, and ID. For longer walks, add water and maybe a small snack. A belt bag keeps everything accessible without the annoyance of bouncing pockets or holding things in your hands.

The Bottom Line

Walking isn't glamorous. There's no "transformation Tuesday" or dramatic before/after. But it's the exercise I've done consistently for over a year now - which is more than I can say for any gym, class, or program I've tried.

These three products have made walking more comfortable and safer, which makes me more likely to do it. That's really all it takes - small things that remove friction and make the habit just a little easier to maintain.

Start small. Go today. See what happens.
