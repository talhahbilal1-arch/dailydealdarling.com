My bathroom counter was a graveyard of half-used products, expired medications, and that moisturizer I bought in 2019 and never opened. Under the sink? Don't even look there. Products stacked on products, everything falling over when you reached for one thing.

Small bathrooms are challenging, but they force you to be intentional. These three organizers helped me maximize every inch of space while keeping daily essentials accessible.

Organizing a Small Bathroom

Tip #1: Use vertical space
Your under-sink cabinet has unused vertical space. Stackable organizers double your storage without taking more footprint.
Tip #2: Counter is for daily use only
If you don't use it every day, it doesn't belong on the counter. Everything else goes in a cabinet or drawer.
Tip #3: Clear containers win
You need to see what you have or you'll forget about it (and buy duplicates). Clear storage prevents mystery product accumulation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I organize a bathroom with no storage?

Use vertical space: over-toilet shelving, door-back organizers, under-sink tiers. Also minimize - you probably don't need half the products taking up space.

What should I keep on my bathroom counter?

Only items you use daily: soap, toothbrush, maybe skincare. Everything else should be stored in cabinets or drawers.

How do I organize under bathroom sink with pipes?

Expandable shelves are made to work around pipes. Also use stackable bins to create tiers of storage.

How often should I declutter bathroom products?

Every 3-6 months, check expiration dates and toss anything you haven't used. Products expire and clutter accumulates fast.

The Bottom Line

Small bathrooms require creativity, not giving up. Under-sink organizers, counter containers, and door storage use space you're probably wasting right now. A few strategic products can transform a cramped bathroom into one that actually works.
