The Sunday Morning Reset Routine That Changed My Week

By Mia Reynolds · December 31, 2024 · 7 min read

Sundays used to fill me with dread - the "Sunday scaries" were real. I'd spend the whole day anxious about Monday while also trying to cram in errands and cleaning. By Sunday night I was exhausted instead of refreshed.

Now I protect Sunday mornings like they're sacred. A few hours of intentional slowness sets the tone for the whole week. These products help me create a reset ritual that actually feels restorative.

Making Sundays Actually Restful

Tip #1: No obligations before noon
Block off Sunday mornings for slow time. No errands, no cleaning, no productivity. Just rest and recharge.
Tip #2: Create sensory cues
Specific candles, drinks, or activities that signal "this is rest time." Your brain learns to associate these cues with relaxation.
Tip #3: Prep for the week without stress
A calm Sunday afternoon of light prep (meal planning, outfit laying) prevents Monday chaos without ruining your rest day.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I stop feeling anxious on Sundays?

Create a restful morning ritual and do light prep in the afternoon so Monday feels less overwhelming. Structure the day around recharging, not dreading.

What should a Sunday reset include?

Whatever helps YOU recharge: slow coffee/tea, reading, light movement, a nice meal. The goal is feeling restored, not checking boxes.

When should I meal prep on Sundays?

Afternoon, after your restorative morning. Protect the morning for rest, then use calm afternoon energy for light prep.

How do I protect Sunday mornings with kids?

Involve them in a slow ritual (pancakes, reading time) or trade off with a partner. Even 30 minutes of protected slow time helps.

The Bottom Line

Sunday mornings aren't for catching up on everything you didn't do all week. They're for refilling your cup so you can face the week ahead. A nice kettle, a cozy blanket, and a good book are all you really need.
