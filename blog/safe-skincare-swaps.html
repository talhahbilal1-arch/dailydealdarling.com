When I got pregnant, I had to toss half my skincare. Retinol? Gone. Salicylic acid? Maybe not. Everything had conflicting information online. Finding pregnancy-safe products that actually worked was frustrating - I didn't want to sacrifice my skin for 9+ months.

These products are considered safe during pregnancy AND actually deliver results. Always confirm with your doctor, but these were the swaps that worked for me.

Navigating Pregnancy Skincare

Avoid during pregnancy:
Retinoids (Retin-A, retinol), high-dose salicylic acid, hydroquinone, chemical sunscreens (some doctors say), certain essential oils. When in doubt, ask your OB.
Generally safe:
Azelaic acid, glycolic acid (low concentrations), vitamin C, hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, mineral sunscreens, most plant-based oils.
Always check with your doctor:
This article is not medical advice. Your provider knows your specific situation best.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is retinol safe during pregnancy?

No - retinoids are generally advised against during pregnancy. Switch to alternatives like azelaic acid or vitamin C for similar benefits.

Can I use salicylic acid while pregnant?

Low concentrations in face washes are generally considered okay, but high-concentration products should be avoided. Ask your doctor.

What sunscreen is safe during pregnancy?

Mineral sunscreens with zinc oxide or titanium dioxide are considered safest. Some doctors okay chemical sunscreens too - ask yours.

Can I prevent stretch marks with products?

Genetics play the biggest role. Keeping skin moisturized helps comfort but no product guarantees prevention. Hydrate and hope for the best.

The Bottom Line

Pregnancy skincare doesn't mean giving up on your skin. These products are considered safe and actually work. Always verify with your provider, but these swaps got me through pregnancy with comfortable, healthy skin.
