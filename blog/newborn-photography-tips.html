DIY Newborn Photos That Don't Look Amateur

By Mia Reynolds · December 31, 2024 · 7 min read

Professional newborn photography costs $300-800+ for a session. I get it - those photos are gorgeous. But with a few simple props and natural light, you can capture beautiful newborn photos at home that you'll treasure forever.

These affordable tools helped me take photos that people assumed were professional. The secret? Good light, simple backgrounds, and a few key props.

Tips for Beautiful At-Home Newborn Photos

Tip #1: Natural light is everything
Shoot near a large window with indirect light. No flash needed. Soft natural light is what makes newborn photos look dreamy.
Tip #2: Keep baby warm and fed
A warm, full baby is a sleepy baby. Sleepy babies are the easiest to photograph. Time your session after a feeding.
Tip #3: Simple backgrounds work best
A plain blanket or wrap in neutral colors keeps focus on baby. Don't over-complicate with too many props.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I take newborn photos?

Within the first 2 weeks while baby is still super sleepy and curled up. After that, they're more alert and less likely to stay posed.

Do I need a professional camera?

A smartphone with good natural light can work great. Portrait mode helps blur backgrounds. Focus on lighting over equipment.

What's the best time of day for photos?

When your window gets the best soft, indirect light - usually mid-morning or late afternoon, depending on which way your windows face.

How do I get baby to stay asleep for photos?

Feed them right before, keep the room warm, use white noise, and work during their sleepiest time. Patience is key.

The Bottom Line

You don't need to spend hundreds on professional newborn photos. Good light, simple props, and a sleepy baby are all you need. These affordable tools help you capture memories that look way more polished than typical snapshots.
