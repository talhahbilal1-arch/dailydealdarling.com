Why Drinking Water Is So Hard

Tip #1: Keep water visible
If your bottle is in your bag or across the room, you won't drink from it. Keep it on your desk, on the counter, wherever you spend time. Out of sight = out of mind.
Tip #2: Make it taste good
If plain water bores you, that's valid. Add lemon, cucumber, or use an infuser. You're more likely to drink something you actually enjoy.
Tip #3: Use drinking cues
Link water to existing habits. Every time you check email? Drink. Before every meal? Drink. After every bathroom break? Drink. Build it into your day.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should I drink daily?

A general guideline is 64oz (eight 8oz cups), but needs vary based on body size, activity level, climate, and individual factors. Start with 64oz as a baseline and adjust based on how you feel. Your urine should be pale yellow.

Do fruit infuser bottles help you drink more water?

For many people, yes. If plain water bores you, adding natural fruit flavor makes it more appealing. The key is finding what makes YOU actually drink. Some people need flavor, others need a bottle they love carrying.

Are insulated tumblers worth the price?

If you prefer cold water, absolutely. Quality insulated bottles keep ice frozen for 24+ hours. Warm water sitting on your desk is less appealing to drink than ice-cold water. The investment pays off in increased hydration.

What size water bottle is best?

32-40oz hits a sweet spot for most people - big enough to reduce refills throughout the day, small enough to carry comfortably. Bigger isn't always better if it's too heavy or bulky to bring with you everywhere.
