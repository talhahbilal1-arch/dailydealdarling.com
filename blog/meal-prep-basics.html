Meal Prep for People Who Hate Meal Prep

By Mia Reynolds · December 31, 2024 · 7 min read

I've tried meal prep approximately 47 times. Usually I'd spend 4 hours on Sunday making elaborate recipes I found on Pinterest, eat the same sad chicken and rice for two days, then abandon the whole thing by Wednesday when I couldn't face another identical container.

The meal prep that finally stuck? It's boring. It's basic. And it takes me about 90 minutes. The secret wasn't better recipes - it was better tools that made the annoying parts (chopping, portioning, storing) fast enough that I'd actually do it.

Why Most Meal Prep Fails

Tip #1: Prep ingredients, not meals
Chopped veggies, cooked grains, and prepped proteins can be combined differently each day. Way less boring than eating the same exact meal five times.
Tip #2: Remove the annoying parts
If chopping vegetables takes forever, you won't do it. A good vegetable chopper cuts prep time in half and makes the process way less tedious.
Tip #3: Invest in good containers
Cheap containers stain, leak, and make your food look sad. Good glass containers make meal prep feel less depressing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does meal prep actually take?

With the right tools, 1-2 hours on Sunday can prep lunches for the whole week. A good vegetable chopper cuts prep time dramatically - what used to take 30 minutes of chopping takes 5.

Are glass containers better than plastic?

Glass doesn't stain from tomato sauce, doesn't retain odors, and is microwave-safe without concerns about chemicals leaching. They're heavier and can break, but they last much longer and keep food looking appetizing.

Do I need a food scale for meal prep?

Not required, but helpful for portion consistency and accurate recipe following. Digital scales are cheap and eliminate guesswork. Especially useful if you're tracking macros or want consistent portions.

How do I keep meal prep from getting boring?

Prep ingredients, not identical full meals. Chopped veggies, cooked proteins, and grains can be combined differently each day with different sauces and seasonings. Much less monotonous than eating the exact same thing five days in a row.

The Bottom Line

Meal prep doesn't have to be a 4-hour Pinterest project. With tools that make the tedious parts fast, it becomes something you'll actually do consistently. These three items removed the friction that made me quit every other time.
